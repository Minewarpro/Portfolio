● Game Concept

The concept of this game is simple yet original. We play in first person in a mystical universe. It's a fast-paced FPS but with the particularity of bullet hell. That means there's a large number of projectiles on the screen, formed with specific patterns. The goal is to dodge them while killing enemies.

● The Mouvement

A lot of polish

Regarding the movement, I spent most of my time on polish.

I did a lot of testing and back-and-forth to create the best possible gamefeel.

Dash & Slide

The dash is perhaps the movement ability that the player will use the most during their game. Therefore, it is crucial that it is well thought out.

Camera Shake

It's no secret that camera shake is essential for immersion in the game. That's why I've used it on all the movements in the game.

Here's an example of camera shake during landing. It varies in intensity depending on the duration of the fall.

● The Weapons

There are two weapons available.

Each of the two weapons underwent the same polish process as the movement :

- Camera

- Animations

- VFX

Vajra

- The first one is the Vajra => Short-range | High area damage | Low fire rate

This weapon is a high-risk, high-reward melee tool, perfect for quickly eliminating swarmers.

Chakram

- The second one is the Chakram => Long-range | Single target | High fire rate

This weapon encourages the player to keep their distance. It's much more comfortable for dealing with bullet hell situations and ranged enemies.

● The Bullet Hell

The main challenge for the bullet hell was performance optimization.

Bullet Pooling System

The first solution for performance was to create a pooling system.

That means instead of spawning and destroying bullets each time, we spawn a large amount at Begin Play and simply teleport them to the right position when needed.
