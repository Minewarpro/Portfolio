● Game Concept

The concept of this game is simple yet original. We play in first person in a mystical universe. It's a fast-paced FPS but with the particularity of bullet hell. That means there's a large number of projectiles on the screen, formed with specific patterns. The goal is to dodge them while killing enemies.

● 3C

The keyword for the 3Cs is "intentions." We have chosen to create a very dynamic game focused on movement.

To address this issue, we have chosen to offer a wide range of movement options available to the player, as shown in the GIF below.

Dash

The dash is perhaps the movement ability that the player will use the most during their game. Therefore, it is crucial that it is well thought out.

We opted for two charges to allow the player to choose whether to consume both in succession to go faster or to use only one for more precision.

The dash is only on the horizontal axis but can move sideways or forwards/backwards to allow the player to more easily dodge projectiles.

Slide

The slide is the ability to move that allows you to gain the most speed in the game.

If you slide down a slope, it continues your slide until the end of it.

If you jump during the slide, you gain a speed and height boost on your jump. It's useful for experienced players who want to optimize their movement

The slide is useful for fast movement, but it can be punishing if used incorrectly in combat. However, it can also be an asset for dodging certain projectile patterns.

● The Player

Managing the player was also a challenge. In Unreal Engine, the default character is derived from the "Character" class, which uses "Character Movement". The problem with this is that it ignores physical constraints that are not predefined within it. Unfortunately, the system mentioned earlier did not work in this case.

The solution I found is to use the parent class "Pawn" and employ another movement management system, "FloatingPawnMovement", which is much more permissive.

The counterparts

The counterpart of this is that there are many features that don't exist, and we have to code them ourselves.

For example, we could mention the management of gravity, the Jump function, or even the On Landed event, which allows the detection of the player when they touch the ground.
