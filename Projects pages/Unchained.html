Details

● The Ball and Chain

Physics Constraints

The chain is attached to the player, and the ball is attached to the chain. Each restricts the other element.

The chain is very stable and doesn't pass through obstacles despite the constraints applied to it.

The ball and the chain can interact with any other physical element without any issues.

The technical challenge

What has been our biggest concern are the forces applied to the chain. Let me explain, if the chain experiences excessive opposing forces from both ends, the links would separate, causing physical and visual glitches.

But by using a rig and adjusting the values, we managed to achieve a very stable chain.

Chain Rig

The chain is entirely physics-based. After numerous tests, the rig seems to be the best option for achieving a stable chain without any glitches.

The rig allows for the adjustment of various parameters, such as weight, damping, and inertia tensor.

Rectangular collisions are used because they work best with the other elements of gameplay physics.

● What can we do with this system ?

Thanks to this stability, the player can hang from or grab the ball to pull on it without it breaking !

Feeling of constraint

The player is limited in their movements when the chain is taut.

To achieve this result, you simply need to increase the damping of the ball when it's on the ground, which prevents the ball from moving too quickly, and consequently, the player as well.

● A little bit of easy fun ?

The player has the ability to grab any physical element, including their ball. We thought that throwing the ball to break things was a good idea.

In the game, all elements with the weathered wood texture can be broken by sending our ball towards them.

It is also possible to grab crates to throw and break them.

● The Player

Managing the player was also a challenge. In Unreal Engine, the default character is derived from the "Character" class, which uses "Character Movement". The problem with this is that it ignores physical constraints that are not predefined within it. Unfortunately, the system mentioned earlier did not work in this case.

The solution I found is to use the parent class "Pawn" and employ another movement management system, "FloatingPawnMovement", which is much more permissive.

The counterparts

The counterpart of this is that there are many features that don't exist, and we have to code them ourselves.

For example, we could mention the management of gravity, the Jump function, or even the On Landed event, which allows the detection of the player when they touch the ground.
