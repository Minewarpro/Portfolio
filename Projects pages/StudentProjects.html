The Maze

"Brave all the mini-games to find the exit !"
Project Type University
Software Used Visual Studio
Languages Used C++

Gameplay

Details

For this project, we had to create a game without using a graphical library. Therefore, we decided to make a maze using ASCII squares.

We were several people on this project. On my part, I took care of :

● From the 3Cs. That is, the player's movement, including the squares they can or cannot move to, as well as the health point system.

● The fog system revealing as the player moves.

● The inventory system and the functionality of items.

● Part of the mini-games. (The ones presented below are only the ones I worked on)

● Game rules

The maze section

To finish the game, you just need to find the exit marked by the green square. Unfortunately, it's dark, and you have to explore to reveal the path little by little.

You have, to help you, an inventory containing potions and bombs.

The potions allow you to recover health points.

While the bomb allows you to break the squares around you on the horizontal and vertical axes.

The mini-game section

You will encounter blue squares along your path representing the mini-games. If you lose at the mini-game, your number of health points will decrease.

Once the mini-game is finished, you can continue your journey.

If you win, not only will you not lose any health points, but you might also receive a potion or a bomb !

● List of Mini-games

Guess the Price

The rules of this mini-game are very simple. You have 10 attempts to find the exact number chosen by the computer.

After each of your guesses, you will be told whether you are above or below the correct number. It's up to you to make a new guess !

Hangman

This one is a classic mini-game ! The computer selects a word that you don't know, and it's up to you to propose a letter.

If you guess correctly, the letter is revealed, but if you guess wrong 9 times, then you lose the game !

Tic-Tac-Toe

"Tic-Tac-Toe" is also a great classic. Align three crosses before your opponent to win the game !

Draws are considered victories as well.

Remember me

10 seconds, that's the time you have to memorize this sequence of numbers ! Once the time is up, you'll have to rewrite the number you think you've remembered.

If you make a mistake with even just one digit, it's game over !

Tick-tock

This mini-game is a test of speed. You will have 5 seconds to rewrite the displayed word.

Be quick but also precise, a single typo and it's game over !
